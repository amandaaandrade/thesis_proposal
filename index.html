Historical context of Moth.

In the nineteenth century, peppered moths in the uk evolved to be blacker. A black peppered moth is naturally occurring but peppered moths are normally white with black specks on their wings; however, due to domestic coal fires and industrialization, which caused sooty air pollution and, which killed the Tree lichens and blackened trees, this made the white moths more visible to predators as they could not hide on the lichen (Butterfly Conservation). Due to these reasons, the moth is the perfect symbol of resistance to this industrial world.
